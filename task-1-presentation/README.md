## Digital Literacy & Professional Success: A Student's Guide:
It is a concise visual resource designed to help students transition from being passive technology consumers to informed digital creators.The guide is organized into five key pillars of digital competence:

## Digital Literacy Fundamentals:
Defines literacy as the ability to evaluate, create, and communicate within a digital ecosystem. It introduces the CRAAP Test (Currency, Relevance, Authority, Accuracy, and Purpose) as a framework for verifying online information.

## The Student Tech Stack:
Recommends specific tools to amplify productivity, such as Notion or Trello for task management, Zotero for automated citations, and Canva or Adobe Express for professional visuals.

## Safe Internet Practices:
Outlines the "Safe-User" Protocol, which includes activating Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA), using high-entropy passphrases instead of standard passwords, and auditing app permissions to minimize one's digital footprint.

## Professional Online Presence:
Emphasizes treating one's digital footprint as a "living portfolio". It suggests optimizing LinkedIn with a professional bio and using GitHub or Behance to provide tangible evidence of academic and personal projects.

## The Digital Handshake (Email Etiquette):
Focuses on professional communication standards, including the BLUF Method (Bottom Line Up Front), the use of descriptive subject lines, and maintaining tone parity with the recipient.
