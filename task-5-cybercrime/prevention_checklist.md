## Stay Safe Online – Checklist for College Students (India) 
	•	The use of strong and distinct passwords is required for all accounts because users must create passwords that include multiple character types. Your name and date of birth and "123456" should not be used as password elements.
	•	The security of Gmail and Instagram and banking and college portals will improve when users activate 2FA as their authentication method.
	•	The SMS links and WhatsApp forwards should not be clicked by users who receive them from unknown sources especially when those links require users to provide their login information.
	•	Users must check public Wi-Fi networks before connecting because they should not use open Wi-Fi networks in cafes or hostels to access their banking or protected accounts.
	•	The installation of app updates and phone updates protects users from new security threats which emerge in their devices.
	•	Users should restrict their online sharing by preventing themselves from publicly disclosing personal information such as their phone number and address and college ID.
	•	Business owners should know that UPI Safety Tip 1 prohibits them from sharing their UPI PIN or OTP with any person who makes such a request regardless of whether the requester states they work for the bank.
	•	Whenever you enter your UPI PIN you will lose money because UPI Safety Tip 2 states that it works as an outgoing payment. Users should verify payment requests before they give their approval.
	•	Users should download applications exclusively from official stores which include Google Play Store and Apple App Store to prevent their devices from acquiring malware.
	•	Users must log out from all shared computing devices which include library computers and friends laptops after they finish using those devices.
	•	The OLX and Instagram platforms require users to be careful because they should not pay in advance and must confirm the identity of both buyers and sellers before conducting business.
