# CASE STUDY
UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud constitutes one of the most prevalent cybercrimes in India because scammers deceive users into making financial transfers and disclosing private information. In a typical situation, fraudsters use their identity as bank officials or customer service representatives or online marketplace buyers and sellers to deceive people.

A college student who sells his laptop on an online marketplace receives a phone call from a person who claims to be a buyer and will use UPI for payment. The scammer demands that the student enter his UPI PIN to receive a payment after sending a payment request instead of sending money.

The step-by-step process usually involves: (1) contacting the victim through calls/messages, (2) building trust using urgency or fake identities, (3) sending fraudulent payment links or collect requests, and (4) tricking the victim into sharing OTPs or entering their UPI PIN.

The most common victims of fraud include students and elderly people and those who lack knowledge about digital payment systems. The results include financial losses that occur immediately and emotional trauma which people experience and challenges with money recovery that happen when there are reporting delays.

The National Payments Corporation of India (NPCI) guidelines state that users must not disclose their UPI PIN or OTP to any third party. The combination of three elements which include people understanding the system and assessing payment requests and quickly informing about doubtful situations has the power to decrease fraud activities.
